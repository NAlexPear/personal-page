My name is Alex, and I build things.



Maybe not kitchen tables or log cabins (not yet, anyway), but you'll always find me building something. Those somethings that I'm most proud of have been made-from-scratch businesses or programs. I take pride in creating organizations that are useful and meaningful (more on what that means later in my blog).


The desire to build things has taken me a lot of places. The need to build has lead me to create teaching opportunities for musicians and artists, or help to turn a student-run nightclub into a successful event space, or create a place for unlikely athletes to meet their full potential, or build a program that helps aspiring programmers learn the skills that they need to change careers into full-time software development.


That's the most important thing to know about me. Some other less important information:

  • Born in Dallas, TX, raised in Cedar Rapids, IA, educated in Northfield, MN, former resident and life-long fan of Nashville, TN.
  • I currently live in St. Louis with my lady love and a dog that looks like a mix of Yoda, a Wampa, and a Siberian Husky.
  • I was the founding Director of Curriculum, Instruction, and Outreach at Claim Academy, St. Louis's first developer bootcamp.
  • Currently, I work as a freelance web developer and technology education consultant.
